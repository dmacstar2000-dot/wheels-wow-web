# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Drift drivers, car builders, and sponsors in New Zealand's motorsport scene looking to book a photographer/filmmaker for events, rolling shots, livery reveals, or sponsor content. They land on the site after seeing DMAC's work on Instagram or being referred by someone in the scene. They're judging on taste and credibility before they ever read pricing.

## Product Purpose

DMAC Media is a one-person drift photography and film crew based in Hamilton, NZ, operating since 2019. The site exists to convert visitors into bookings. Every page should make the work feel inevitable — the only logical choice for anyone serious about their car or their brand in the NZ drift scene.

## Brand Personality

Premium, cinematic, editorial. The work speaks first; words follow. Not loud — authoritative. Like a magazine spread, not a race day flyer.

## Primary Reference

Larry Chen Photo (larrychenphoto.com) — the benchmark for automotive editorial photography presentation. Key signals: near-black backgrounds, images allowed to breathe at full scale, minimal chrome, typography that steps back and lets photography dominate, restraint as a form of confidence.

## Anti-references

Generic stock-photo portfolio templates. Wedding photographer aesthetic. Flashy esports neon. Overcrowded motorsport event sites. Anything that screams "template."

## Design Principles

1. **The work carries the weight.** Photography is the UI. Every layout decision is about getting out of its way.
2. **Restraint is confidence.** The less the chrome competes with the images, the more premium the site feels.
3. **Editorial over promotional.** Presentation like a magazine spread, not a sales page. Copy is tight; images are generous.
4. **Dark by scene.** This is viewed by people who spend time at night events, in garages, on dark NZ back roads. Dark backgrounds are native to the context.
5. **Credibility through specificity.** Real details (track names, car specs, driver names) over generic taglines. The scene recognizes itself.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Standard good practice. WCAG AA contrast minimums, keyboard navigable, reduced-motion respected.
